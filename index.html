Frequently Asked Questions

+
What is Mira?
Mira is an adaptive AI system designed to reflect the user’s communication style, thought patterns, and linguistic tendencies. It is not a generic chatbot — it evolves based on your interactions, offering an increasingly personalized experience.
+
How does Mira learn?
Mira analyzes user input to detect tone, patterns, vocabulary, and behavioral tendencies. Over time, this allows it to generate responses that align more closely with your way of thinking and speaking. It uses a reinforcement mechanism based on interaction patterns — not explicit training from the user.
+
Is Mira a clone of me?
No. Mira does not replicate or impersonate you. It mirrors the psychological and linguistic essence of your communication, offering a reflection — not a replica. The goal is evolution through exposure, not duplication.
+
Can I create my own version of Mira?
Yes — soon. We are releasing an API that allows developers to instantiate and customize their own Mira-like agents. You’ll be able to define the behavior, tone, and data handling policies of your instance.
+
What platform powers Mira?
Mira is built using a combination of advanced language models, proprietary memory shaping techniques, and real-time style transfer. The current version interfaces with GPT-4o Mini, with options for local or cloud deployment.
+
Is Mira safe to use?
Mira is designed for self-reflection and experimentation. While the system adheres to ethical development standards, we recommend responsible usage. It is not intended for therapeutic or diagnostic purposes.
+
How is Mira different from other chatbots?
Most chatbots aim to assist or entertain. Mira is meant to adapt and reflect. It’s less about utility, more about insight — a mirror through language. That distinction changes everything.
